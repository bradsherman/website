About Me
Introduction

I am a member of the class of 2018 at Notre Dame majoring in Computer Science. I am from Ida, a small town in southeastern Michigan. I came to Notre Dame as a chemical engineer, but started coding in the second semester of my freshman year, switched to computer science, and never looked back. I am always trying to learn more about programming and computers in general. I am especially interested in networking and cyber security. I believe that now more than ever we need to focus on security because soon the "Internet of Things" will surround us, and we must make sure that any item that has a computer in it is secure.

Outside the classroom, I enjoy playing sports. I played basketball and baseball in high school, so I try to play those as much as I can. I also love throwing a football around, or trying out new sports like volleyball or soccer. I'm a Detroit sports fan, and I enjoy watching the Tigers, Lions, Red Wings, and Pistons when I have time. When I'm not playing sports or doing schoolwork, I enjoy watching movies and playing video games with my friends. I am also interested in working on side projects, but since I'm relatively new to computer science, I am learning as much as I can in order to sharpen my skills.
